The manuscript of Joshua Sharpless’s “Journal into Indian Country, (1798) is housed at Friends Historical Library, on the campus of Swarthmore College. In the spring semester of 2012, Swarthmore students enrolled in English 57A (“Early American Literature Through A Traveler’s Eyes”) transcribed Sharpless’s manuscript as part of a larger project to make the library’s rich store of Quaker journal writings more accessible to researchers. The project was designed by Christopher Densmore, curator of Friends Historical Library, and Keat Murray, Visiting Associate Professor of English. Transcribers included: Kara Stoever (3-38), Kari Olmon (39-74), Zach Wunrow (75-87, 153-174), Margaret Duszyk (88-104), Roberto Conreras (105-123), and David Fialkow (124-152). Students’ transcriptions were compiled and edited in the present form by Professor Murray.

The transcribers and editor attempted to render Sharpless’s orthography and grammar as they appear on the page of his journal, even when Sharpless’s own spelling and usage are inconsistent. Because the manuscript does not include page numbers, the transcribers have interpolated page numbers.
