The manuscript of Jonathan Thomas’s journal is housed at Friends Historical Library, on the campus of Swarthmore College. The transcription was part of a larger transcription project completed from October to December 2010 by students at Swarthmore College enrolled in English 50A, “Reading Red, White, and In-Between in Early American Literature.” The project was a collaborative effort between Christopher Densmore, curator of Friends Historical Library, and Keat Murray, Visiting Assistant Professor of English. Transcribers included: Jessica Holler (title, ii, 1-14) and Tiffany Lee (15-28). The transcription was compiled and edited by Professor Murray.

The transcribers and editor attempted to render Jonathon Thomas’s orthography and grammar as they appear on the page of his journal, even when Thomas’s own spelling and usage are inconsistent. Additionally, some questions remain about spelling the names of Thomas’s Quaker acquaintances and individual Native Americans who populate his journal. The transcribers and editor attempted to remain faithful to Thomas’s Anglicized spellings in all cases.

Thomas’ manuscript does, for the most part, include page numbers; the page numbers on the transcription correspond with the manuscript sheets, including Thomas' discontinuous numbering on chronological pages 3 and 4.
